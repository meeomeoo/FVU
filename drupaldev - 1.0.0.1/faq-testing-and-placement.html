Is there a deadline by which I need to complete the placement tests?

Answer: It is best to complete all tests as early as possible. You should complete all required placement testing within two weeks of confirming your Rutgers status.

 I took my tests but I don’t know my results yet - should I still attend APA/advising day?

Answer: Yes! Even if you don’t know your results, you should absolutely attend your advising/APA day.

 

I took my placement tests (or am exempt) but there is no check mark next to placement testing on Enrollment Pathway. What should I do?

Answer: Don’t worry! Enrollment Pathway updates completely a little bit later. To confirm that you’ve submitted required tests successfully, you can log back onto the test site, and scroll down. You’ll see checkmarks next to sections that you’ve submitted for Math and English.

 

How can I confirm whether or not I am exempt from any test?

Answer: Refer to the “Exemptions from Testing” link on http://oirap.rutgers.edu/testplace.htm. If you are exempt, you should not take any portion of the corresponding test - you are exempt from the ENTIRE test. You do not have to inform us of your exemption - simply make sure that you have sent all relevant scores to Rutgers via CollegeBoard. If you want to confirm, please check your CollegeBoard account order history. If you are waiting on any scores, or have not sent them yet, you should take the placement test.

What if I need testing accommodations?

Answer: Before attempting any placement test, you must contact the Office of Disability Services by following this link. Tests can only be attempted once, so please be sure to get in touch with ODS first.

 

Do I need to register or pay for placement tests?

Answer: No! However, you must make sure to pay your acceptance fee to Rutgers University in order to activate your NetID, which allows you to log onto the test.

 

How do I take the tests?

Answer: Read through important information on http://oirap.rutgers.edu/testplace.html, and once you are done, you can click on the links to get you to placement testing. Make sure you only begin to test when you are feeling well and you have ample time.

 

How long are the Math and English tests?

Answer: The Math test is 2 hours long and has three sections, and the English test is 90 minutes long and has four sections. You should leave an extra few minutes to review instructions.

 

Can I take breaks during the test?

Answer: You must complete the Math test sections consecutively in one sitting, and you must complete the English test sections consecutively in one sitting. However, you may take an evening off between the Math and English tests. Once you submit either the Math or English test, you must complete the other test (unless exempt) within 72 hours of the first submission.

 

Can I use calculators, dictionaries, or other aid tools?

Answer: No - do your best independent work so that we get a true measure of your abilities and place you into classes in which you can succeed. An incorrect placement due to purposeful misrepresentation of your score will result in a lower GPA or a delay in your graduation. We want you to succeed and invite you to simply do your best work.
